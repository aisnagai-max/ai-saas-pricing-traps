# AI SaaS Pricing Traps — A Buyer's Checklist

Most AI tool pricing pages are designed to look cheaper than they are.
This is a curated checklist of the recurring tricks — so you can read any
pricing page in 30 seconds and know what you'll actually pay.

Curated and maintained by [aisnag.ai](https://aisnag.ai) — hand-checked, honest AI tool listings.

---

## The 5 most common pricing traps

### 1. Annual price shown as if it's monthly
The big number says "$15/mo" — but the small print says *billed annually*.
Pay month-to-month and it's often 20–40% higher.
**Check:** look for "billed annually" or a monthly/annual toggle near the price.

### 2. The disappearing promo
A "limited time" discount that's been "ending soon" for six months.
The real price is the one without the promo.
**Check:** is the discounted price the headline, or the exception?

### 3. Enterprise-only / "Contact us"
The plan you actually need has no number — just a "Talk to sales" button.
This usually means it's expensive and negotiable.
**Check:** can you see a real price for the tier that fits your use case?

### 4. Dynamic prices that load after the page
Some pages (often built on modern JS frameworks) load the price last, or
A/B-test different numbers. The price you see may not be the price others see.
**Check:** reload the page; compare with an incognito window.

### 5. Per-seat math that hides the real cost
"$9/user" sounds cheap until you multiply by your team size and add the
mandatory minimum seats.
**Check:** what's the real monthly total for *your* actual headcount?

---

## How to read any pricing page in 30 seconds

1. Find the **monthly-billed** price, not the annual-as-monthly number.
2. Ignore any "limited time" framing — assume the promo won't be there.
3. Check whether the tier *you* need has a visible price at all.
4. Multiply per-seat by your real team size, plus minimum seats.
5. If the price isn't verifiable, treat it as "unknown," not "cheap."
